Chronic Crime Behavior through the lens of Mental Health and Other Social Dimensions:
An examination of the 2016 Inmates Survey from the Bureau of Justice Statistics

Mass incarceration represents a public health and social issue in the U.S. In an effort to understand this problem, we examine the rates of recidivism of inmates across several social dimensions such as mental illness prevalence, family conditions, emotional difficulties and education, using the data from the BJS 2016 Inmates Survey.
